The Deadlift

Description

The deadlift is a primarily lower body but can also be classified as a full body compound movement. It is a key excercise when training for strength and like the squat is one of the more physcial and mentally taxing movements. The primary muscles being used for the deadlift are the hamstrings, spinal erectors, and glutes with a respectable amount of activation being shown in the lats, trapezius, and levator scapulae.

Instructions

Step 1: Stand with your feet hip-distance apart and bend down in a sitting motion until your thighs are parallel with the ground while ensuring your back is straight. Take an overhand grip on the bar with your handss slightly wider than shoulder-width aprt.

Step 2: Take a deep breath into your diaphragm and lift the bar by pushing up through your legs imagining your arms are just ropes with a claw at the end.

Step 3: As the bar reaches your knees push your hips forward to raise your torso. The lift is completed when your arms are by your side and the bar is resting on your thigh making sure to not lean back.

Step 4: Exhale while reversing the movement to bring the weight to the ground.
