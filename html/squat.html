The Squat

Description

The squat is an effective excercise for developing lower body strength. Squatting is a compound movement (a movement that utilizes multiple muscle groups) that uses some of the largest muscles in our body which can cause the feeling of fatigue shortly after performing the excercise. While the movement primarily targets the quadriceps the glutes have also been shown to have a large amount of electromyography (EMG) activation during the movement with the soleus playing a primarily balancing role.

Instructions

Step 1: Stand with your feet hip-distance apart and with them facing forward and rest the bar on the back of your shoulders just above your shoulder blades.

Step 2: Brace your core by taking a deep breath into your diaphragm. Begin the movement at your hips through pushing them behind you in a sitting motion then bend your knees ensuring they travel in the direction that your toes are pointing.

Step 3: Travel down until your knees are atleast flexed at a 90 degree angle. If possible travel down to the point that the crease of your hip is below your knees (referred to as breaking parallel). Once at the lowest point in your squat exhale slowly and push your body weight up while making sure to evenly distribute the pressure across your feet.
